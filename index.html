About Me

I'm Kindy Tice, a product of the Midwest although I most recently scratched my California itch for three and a half years. In the Bay Area, I gained valuable work experience in web design and programming and successfully completed a number of courses in both disciplines, most recently Dev Bootcamp, an immersive four month coding bootcamp. As a techno optimist, I can't think of doing anything more exciting with my life. As technology continues to advance exponentially, I will eagerly at the intersection of people and technology with a passion to learn the technologies of today and tomorrow.

What Drives Me

I'm an intrinsically motivated individual who feels most comfortable being part of a team. I naturally form meaningful relationships with coworkers and clients alike because I have no problem rolling up my sleeves and getting the job done. When I've been in positions of leadership, I lead by example from the front lines. I've been fortunate enough to be a part of a number of successful teams and I know that a big part of team success is realizing that the whole is greater than the sum total of its parts.

Why Programming

Coming out of college, it was my natural inclination to help others and be a "man for others." Customer service was a natural fit and my first stop was iStream Financial Services, an electronic payment processing company in the Milwaukee area. At iStream I was exposed to our team of developers, whom I admired personally and professionally. I admired their quiet confidence and the trust the rest of the company had in their team. Working closely with them inspired me to express my creativity and logic reasoning through the medium of development, which ultimately led me to move to San Francisco to learn the craft.

Upon landing in the Bay Area, I enrolled in a number of classes in Ruby, HTML5, JavaScript and web design, just to name a few. I brought my knowledge to the table professionally and did web design work while at Betabrand and Newton Software.After successfully completing Dev Bootcamp, I am eager to transition my career from a reactive problem-solving support technician into a proactive problem solver as a developer.
